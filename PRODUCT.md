# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cybersecurity professionals — primarily blue team defenders, security admins, and compliance practitioners. Chapter leads who organise local meetups. Event organisers and speakers.

The community is Microsoft-adjacent (run by Microsoft MVPs, Partners, Staff, and enthusiasts) but is independently operated and not a Microsoft property.

Context: users come here to find local chapters, register for events, submit talks when CFPs are open, and manage their chapter/event operations via the dashboard.

## Product Purpose

Connect cybersecurity professionals worldwide through local chapters and community events. The platform exists to:

1. Encourage formation of new chapters in underserved regions
2. Drive event attendance — making it effortless to discover, register, and show up
3. Surface CFP opportunities so community members contribute presentations
4. Give chapter leads and admins lightweight tools to run events without friction

Success looks like: growing chapter count, healthy event attendance, and a steady stream of community-contributed talks.

## Brand Personality

Community · Inclusive · Professionally Cybersecurity

- **Community-first**: welcoming, approachable, low barrier to participation
- **Inclusive**: not gatekeeping, not elitist, not "hacker culture" — this is for defenders and builders
- **Professional**: credible and expert without being corporate or stiff

## Anti-references

- Hacker/offensive-security aesthetics (dark terminals, green-on-black matrix rain, skull iconography)
- Anything that looks like an official Microsoft property or corporate portal
- Overly gamified or startup-bro energy
- Generic SaaS templates with hero metrics and gradient cards

## Design Principles

1. **Lower the barrier** — Every interaction should make participation feel easy, not exclusive. Joining a chapter or registering for an event should feel as simple as RSVPing to a friend's meetup.
2. **Show, don't gate** — Surface what's happening (events, chapters, CFPs) without requiring login first. Let the value be visible before asking for commitment.
3. **Expert confidence, not intimidation** — The community IS cybersecurity professionals; the design should feel credible and knowledgeable without being hostile or exclusionary to newcomers.
4. **Community over platform** — The tool stays out of the way. The people, chapters, and events are the stars, not the UI itself.
5. **Earn trust** — As a security community, the site itself should demonstrate good security and accessibility practices. Practice what you preach.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Colour contrast: ≥4.5:1 for body text, ≥3:1 for large text and UI components
- Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Screen reader support: semantic HTML, ARIA labels where needed, skip navigation
- No colour-only indicators — always pair with text/icon
